Note4 Strategies × 2 Levels

Cross-sectional backtests on 660 individual stocks (S&P 500 + STOXX 600 majors), compared with factor-level implementations on 5 Fama-French factors. Two signals: MAX (highest single-day return) and DRIF (elastic net on 42 daily return features).

Stock-level: Each month, rank all 660 stocks by signal, long top decile (~38 stocks), short bottom decile. Monthly rebalance.

Factor-level: Each month, rank 5 factors by signal, long top 2. Monthly rebalance.

4-strategy comparison. Y: growth of $1 (log scale). Stock-level uses D1 long / D10 short across 660 stocks. Factor-level uses top-2 of 5 FF factors. Dashed line = OOS start (2020). Source: HuggingFace equity_daily + FF5/Momentum via hd_factors().

TipWhat the Comparison Shows

  1. Factor DRIF has the best risk-adjusted full-period Sharpe (0.26) — elastic net on factor daily returns produces the most stable signal

  2. Stock DRIF has the best OOS Sharpe (0.79) — the pooled elastic net generalises well to unseen data, even though in-sample returns are weak

  3. Stock MAX has the highest raw returns (12.1% CAGR) but with extreme volatility (44%) and drawdown (-74%). The signal is real but noisy.

  4. Factor MAX is the weakest — a single extreme daily return at the factor level contains less cross-sectional information than at the stock level

  5. The DRIF paper’s insight is confirmed: the full daily return distribution (42 features) captures more predictive information than the single maximum (1 feature), especially out-of-sample

  • Why OOS > IS?

Stock-level DRIF. Pooled elastic net (alpha=0.5, 5-fold CV) on 42 features per stock-month (21 chronological + 21 rank daily returns). D1 long / D10 short. Expanding window with 60-month minimum.

WarningIn-Sample Weakness, Out-of-Sample Strength

Stock-level DRIF shows an unusual pattern: OOS Sharpe (0.79) is much higher than IS Sharpe (-0.02). Possible explanations:

  1. Elastic net regularisation is very aggressive with 42 features × 400 stocks — the model learns slowly, needing many years before the signal emerges

  2. Universe composition changed — more stocks entered the dataset post-2010, improving the cross-sectional signal quality

  3. Regime shift — the post-2020 period (COVID recovery, meme stocks, factor rotation) may coincidentally favour the DRIF signal

  4. Small OOS window — only 62 months of OOS data; results may not be robust

This warrants further investigation: split OOS into sub-periods, test with different expanding window minimums, and compare with a random signal baseline.
