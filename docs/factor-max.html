NoteFactor MAX Rotation

Based on Alpha Architect research (Dec 2025): the maximum daily return within a month predicts next-month factor returns. This signal captures investor underreaction to factor-level news and is distinct from factor momentum.

Implementation: Each month, rank Fama-French factors by their MAX signal (highest single-day return that month). Go long the top 2 factors next month with equal weights. Rebalance monthly.

TipThe MAX Signal

For each factor, each month:

  1. Compute MAX: the single highest daily return in that month
  2. Rank factors by MAX (1 = highest)
  3. Next month: go long the top-2 ranked factors with equal weights

Intuition: A factor with a high MAX day signals that the factor’s underlying theme (value, momentum, etc.) is receiving positive attention. Investors underreact to this signal, creating predictable returns.

Key distinction from momentum: Factor momentum uses average or cumulative returns. MAX uses only the single best day. The Alpha Architect study shows these capture different information — MAX remains predictive even after controlling for momentum.

  • Key Insight

Academic factors (HML, Mom) are long-short portfolios constructed from individual stocks. Factor ETFs (VLUE, MTUM) are tradeable products that attempt to capture the same premiums but differ in construction, costs, and tracking.

WarningETFs ≠ Academic Factors

The low correlation between factor ETFs and their academic counterparts (VLUE/HML = 0.34, MTUM/Mom = -0.09) means:

  1. Factor MAX applied to ETFs would produce different results than the academic backtest shown on the Strategy page
  2. ETFs include market beta — academic factors are market-neutral long-short portfolios
  3. ETFs have tracking error, fees, and capacity constraints that academic factors ignore
  4. MTUM and Mom are negatively correlated — the iShares momentum ETF does not track the Fama-French momentum factor

A practical implementation would need to decide: rotate among ETFs using ETF-derived MAX signals, or use academic factor signals to select ETFs?
