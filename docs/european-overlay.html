The Composite Indicator of Systemic Stress (CISS) is the ECB’s flagship risk indicator, published daily since 2000. It measures systemic stress across 5 financial market segments:

Sub-market What it captures Key variable
Equity Stock market volatility + returns Realised vol, max drawdown, index returns
Bond Government bond spreads + volatility Yield spread, realised vol
FX Currency volatility EUR exchange rate volatility
Money market Interbank stress EURIBOR-OIS spread, bid-ask
Financial intermediary Bank/insurance sector Bank stock vol, CDS spreads

The composite index combines all 5 using time-varying cross-correlations — when markets stress simultaneously (contagion), the index spikes more than the sum of parts.

Range: 0 (calm) to 1 (systemic crisis). Peak: 0.84 during the GFC (Oct 2008).

Why CISS, not VSTOXX? VSTOXX (European implied vol, equivalent to VIX) is a commercial Qontigo product with no free API. CISS equity sub-index correlates r=0.75 with VIX and is freely available from the ECB.

Source: ECB SDMX API — series CISS/D.U2.Z0Z.4F.EC.SS_CIN.IDX. R function: hd_ecb(). Methodology: Holló, Kremer & Lo Duca (2012), ECB WP #1426.

The CISS overlay reduces risk through regime-dependent exposure scaling:

Regime CISS equity level Equity exposure When it fires
Benign Below 33rd percentile 100% Normal markets — fully invested
Cautious 33rd-67th percentile 50% Elevated stress — half equity, half cash
Hostile Above 67th percentile 10% Crisis — near-cash, avoid drawdown
Return attribution:
  • Return drag in calm markets: CISS overlay slightly underperforms buy & hold because cautious regime reduces exposure even when markets are fine (STOXX 600: 5.0% vs 10.6% CAGR)
  • Drawdown protection: Max DD halved across all ETFs (-36% to -43% buy & hold → -8% to -14% CISS overlay)
  • Sharpe improvement: Lower vol more than compensates for lower CAGR in 4 of 5 ETFs (Euro Stoxx 50: Sharpe 1.03 vs 0.56)
  • STOXX 600 exception: Broad diversification already provides some crisis protection, so the overlay’s cost (reduced upside) exceeds its benefit (reduced drawdown) on a Sharpe basis
Why CISS works better than VIX for European equities:
  1. CISS is contemporaneous — it measures European stress directly, not a lagged US signal
  2. CISS captures bond, FX, and money market stress that VIX misses (pure equity vol)
  3. CISS cross-correlation component detects contagion — markets moving together in crisis
